The Puchi Olympics: What are They?

Source: trust me bro

The Puchi Olympics are held on a random day of each year. This is decided by the first puchi to circle a date on a calender of next year. In fact, the 1990 and 1991 olympics occured back to back, with the 1990 olympics occuring on 12/31, and the 1991 olympics occuring on 1/1.

There are 3 sports in the Puchi Olympics: basketball, swimming, and the 200 foot dash.

The first sport is the 200 foot dash, which only one puchi does. Below is a short clip of Mean Puchi getting stuck while doing the 200 foot dash.

The next sport the puchis compete in is basketball. This is always red puchis vs blue puchis. Recently, Big Puchi has taken up the role of ref, and he calls fouls that he makes. Additionally, some puchis have begun to bring Canadian flags and rubber ducks to the events. The reason why is unknown, but see if you can find the Canadian flag in the clip below, a shot of the puchis warming up before the basketball game.

The last sport the puchis compete in is swimming. This sport usually takes place at the most popular puchi beach, named "Puchi Beach #3.14" (wow). Here the puchis gather, where the first 3 puchis to wade into the water have to swim. The smallest puchi always gets to ride a massive rubber duck, which prevents an unfair advantage from the bigger puchis. Swimming is about 500 feet in length. Below is a short clip of the puchis wading in the water, trying to reach the finish. They are around the 150 foot mark.

The flags are made by the puchi spectators in the event. They are not team related, the puchis just draw them so they can wave funny looking cloth. The Canadian flags are stolen from Canada, and are almost always returned. The flags don't actually do anything, the puchis just like them.

The puchis all gather around a giant mango, and whoever can eat the most wins the Puchi Olympics (even if they didn't participate in a single sport). Why do they do the sports?
